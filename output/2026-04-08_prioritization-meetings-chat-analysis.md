# Chat Analysis: 2026-04-08

**Source:** Microsoft Teams Chat (feedback on prioritization and planning meetings)
**Date:** 2026-04-08 to 2026-04-10
**Exported:** 2026-04-10
**Context:** The chat collects feedback on recent prioritization meetings, especially around stakeholder alignment, documentation of decisions, meeting purpose, and how to discuss impact.

---

## Questions & Answers

**Q: How have the first prioritization meetings been experienced so far, and what should be changed?**
A: Feedback was mixed but fairly consistent on the main issues. Participants said stakeholders do not always interpret the meeting in the same way, which weakens the meaning of agreed priorities. They also wanted clearer documentation of decisions, more discussion about KPIs, impact, and uplift, and a better way to represent discovery work. One participant from the Platform Domain said the meetings are currently efficient and useful for shared understanding, but not really used to resolve direct priority conflicts.

---

**Q: Are the right stakeholders participating in these meetings?**
A: For B2C topics, Chris and Lina were seen as the relevant stakeholders. For possible B2B aspects in the Profiles Team, there may be gaps. Another participant said they still do not understand the organization well enough to judge whether anyone important is missing.

---

**Q: How could meeting decisions be documented better than with a Miro snapshot?**
A: One suggestion was to enable a meeting transcript and let AI generate a decision log or summary from it.
**Q: Could this be piloted immediately?**
A: Yes. The suggestion was that it could be piloted the same day.

---

**Q: Is the main problem the tooling in Miro, or are other issues more important?**
A: Miro itself was described as workable, but the cards were seen as visually overloaded. Another participant explicitly said this is the lowest priority compared with the more important issues around alignment, meaning, and outcome clarity.

---

**Q: Should the meetings focus more on KPIs, impact, and uplift?**
A: Yes. That concern was strongly shared. At the same time, one response noted that transparency is also an explicit goal of these meetings, and in the Platform Domain deep impact comparisons can feel like overhead when priorities are already largely set by company programs.

## Additional Information

- **Stakeholder commitment risk:** One participant said that if stakeholders do not take the meeting seriously and interpret prioritization differently, the rest of the exercise loses value.
- **Meeting purpose ambiguity:** Several messages suggest the meetings currently sit somewhere between prioritization, transparency, and status updates, which creates confusion.
- **Platform Domain view:** In Platform, the meetings were described as quick and efficient, mostly creating transparency and shared understanding rather than settling trade-offs on the spot.
- **Business case usage:** There was one example where a business case from ACTs was brought in and pitched, but it was not compared against equivalent business cases for other initiatives.
- **Discovery visibility problem:** Discovery work consumes time, including engineering time, and people want a clearer way to show stakeholders that this work is progressing even though it is not in the same status as normal initiatives.
