# Proposal to Improve the Domain Priority Meetings

**Audience:** VP Product
**Status:** Discussion draft
**Created:** 2026-04-21

## Executive Summary

Feedback on the first Domain Priority Meetings shows that the format is useful in principle, but not yet stable enough in its current form. The core issue is not `Miro` or a single meeting detail, but an unclear operating model: the format is currently experienced partly as a prioritization round, partly as a transparency sync, and partly as a status update. This creates different expectations, unclear decision impact, and avoidable friction after the meeting.

## What the feedback makes clear

1. **The purpose of the format is not clearly defined.**
   The meeting currently sits between prioritization, transparency, and status readout. As long as that is not decided explicitly, agenda, participant logic, and expectations remain blurred.

2. **Decision commitment is too weak.**
   Several comments show that priorities agreed in the meeting can later be softened or reinterpreted when key stakeholders or dependent areas were not fully aligned.

3. **The decision basis is not consistent enough.**
   Stakeholders expect more clarity on business value, KPIs, trade-offs, and dependencies. Without consistent inputs, prioritization remains hard to compare across initiatives.

4. **Preparation and follow-up communication are still too costly.**
   Stakeholders sometimes come to the meetings insufficiently prepared, and after the meeting it is not always clear what was decided and how that clarity should flow back into the organization.

## Core idea: introducing a Business One-Pager

The Business One-Pager is a short, standardized information document for topics that should be discussed and prioritized in the Domain Priority Meetings. It describes an initiative in a consistent structure. At its core, it captures the problem, the proposed solution, the expected outcome including relevant planning metrics, and the key dependencies. It is a condensed working basis for a prioritization-relevant discussion.

## Why the one-pager is the right lever

The `one-pager` addresses several issues at the same time:

- It gives all initiatives a consistent structure in a business-oriented format.
- It increases the chance that stakeholders prepare for the meetings on the basis of the `one-pager`.
- It improves comparability across initiatives.
- It reduces duplicate work when later artifacts are derived from the same information, for example Marketing Sheets.

## Proposal for alignment with VP Product

I would focus the discussion on four management questions:

1. **How do we describe the binding purpose of the Domain Priority Meetings?**
   A true prioritization round, a transparency format, or a hybrid with clear rules?

2. **When is a decision from this format binding?**
   Which stakeholders must be represented, and how do we deal with missing key participants or conflicting priorities? Do specific stakeholders have veto rights?

3. **What is the minimum decision basis we expect before a prioritization discussion?**
   What information must be available per topic so that a comparable decision is possible at all?

4. **Should the one-pager become the mandatory standard input artifact for new or prioritization-relevant topics?**
   If yes, it should not be treated as just another document, but as a binding part of the meeting process.

## Recommended direction

I would recommend the following next steps:

- Position the Domain Priority Meetings clearly as a prioritization-oriented format with a defined information component.
- Invite `PlatOps` to all rounds.
- Introduce the `one-pager` as the mandatory standard input artifact for new or prioritization-relevant topics.
- Set a clear expectation that stakeholders should come prepared.
- Prepare decisions through a simple, transparent set of criteria instead of debating mainly from instinct in the meeting, for example based on expected outcome.
- Shift to `Jira` as the overview layer in order to reduce inconsistencies between `Miro` and `Jira` and to reduce duplicate work.
- After each meeting, maintain a clear result artifact that makes decisions and follow-up actions visible.

In the meeting, we use `Jira` as the overview across all topics and as the moderation guide; for the topics in `to prioritize`, the substantive basis is provided in advance through the `one-pager`.

The same principle should apply here as well: Inspect & Adapt.
